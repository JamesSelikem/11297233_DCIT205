Insight Into Undergraduate Programmes

Undergraduate Courses

Computer Science

Level 100

The Computer Science Department offers a comprehensive curriculum for students at the 100 level, equipping them with foundational skills and knowledge. In the first semester, courses such as Critical Thinking and Practical Reasoning, Algebra and Trigonometry, and Introduction to Computer Science pave the way for a well-rounded education. The second semester builds on this foundation with Academic Writing, Understanding Human Society, and Programming courses. Students delve into subjects like Calculus, Statistics, and Computer Hardware Fundamentals, gaining a diverse skill set.

Level 200

In the second year, our Computer Science Department offers an engaging curriculum designed to deepen students' understanding and practical skills. The first semester includes Academic Writing II, Programming I, and courses in Digital Systems Design and Multimedia. Students delve into Computer Architecture, Organisation, and advanced Calculus, laying a robust foundation for their technical journey. The second semester introduces Mobile Application Development, Data Structures & Algorithm I, and courses focusing on Systems Administration and Software Engineering. Numerical and Computational Methods round off the semester, ensuring a well-rounded education that prepares students for the challenges and opportunities in the dynamic field of computer science.

Level 300

In the third year, the Computer Science Department introduces advanced courses aimed at honing specialized skills. The first semester includes pivotal subjects such as Operating Systems, Computer Networks, and Database Fundamentals, alongside Introduction to Artificial Intelligence and Discrete Mathematics. Students have the option to choose from electives like Mini-Project, Embedded Systems, and IoT, and Machine Learning. The second semester offers a range of electives, including a comprehensive project, addressing Social, Legal, Ethical, and Professional Issues, and exploring topics like Image Processing, Cloud Computing, and Computational Models for Social Media Mining.

Level 400

In the final year, our Computer Science Department provides a culminating experience through a capstone Project, fostering a synthesis of knowledge and skills. Students engage with critical aspects of Social, Legal, Ethical, and Professional Issues in computing. The semester also includes specialized courses in Image Processing, and a range of elective offerings such as Designing Intelligent Agents, Statistical Models for Data Science, and Network Performance Analysis. In the second semester, the Project continues, complemented by courses in Management Principles, Systems and Network Security, and Wireless Systems. Elective options further enrich students' learning, covering Advanced Databases, Computer Vision, Data Mining, and various advanced topics in computer networks, compilers, and telecommunications.

Undergraduate Courses

Information Technology

Level 100

In the foundational year at the 100 level, students in our Computer Science Department embark on a diverse academic journey. The first semester encompasses essential courses like Critical Thinking, Introduction to Computer Science, and Mathematics for IT Professionals, fostering a well-rounded understanding of the field. Students have the option to choose from a variety of electives, including Algebra and Trigonometry, Vectors and Geometry, and Introduction to Economics. The second semester introduces Academic Writing, Understanding Human Society, and advanced mathematical concepts like Calculus and Algebra. Programming and Computer Hardware Fundamentals round out the semester, laying a solid foundation for students as they progress in their studies.

Level 200

In the second year, the Computer Science Department offers a dynamic curriculum aimed at advancing students' skills and knowledge. The first semester includes Academic Writing II, Programming I, and courses in Digital and Logic Systems Design, Multimedia, and Web Design. Students explore the intricacies of Computer Architecture & Organization and E-Business Architectures, gaining a holistic perspective. The second semester introduces Mobile Application Development, Data Structures & Algorithm I, and courses focusing on Systems Administration and Software Engineering. Electives, including opportunities for Internship, Numerical and Computational Methods, and Information Modeling, allow students to tailor their learning experience and prepare for real-world applications in the field of computer science.

Level 300

In the third year, the Computer Science Department introduces advanced courses aimed at cultivating specialized skills.The first semester covers Operating Systems, Computer Networks, and Database Fundamentals, providing students with a robust understanding of core concepts. Introduction to Artificial Intelligence and IT Project Management further enrich the curriculum. Students have the flexibility to choose electives such as Mini-Project, Principles of 3D Environment, and Software Evolution. The second semester delves into Human-Computer Interaction, Research Methods, and advanced topics like Data Structures and Algorithms II and Information Security Management. Programming II is complemented by elective offerings including Game Engine Architecture and Database Management Administration, ensuring a well-rounded education tailored to students' interests and career goals.

Level 400

In the final year, the Computer Science Department offers an intensive curriculum, placing emphasis on practical application and specialization. The first semester includes a comprehensive Project, addressing Social, Legal, Ethical, and Professional Issues, and exploring Digital Forensics. Elective options like Play and Games, Advanced Software Engineering, Agile Methods, and Persuasive Systems Development allow students to tailor their studies. The second semester continues with the Project, complemented by courses in Management Principles, Systems and Network Security, and Wireless Systems. A range of electives covering Advanced Databases, Computer Vision, and Digital Signal Processing ensures students graduate with advanced skills and a deep understanding of cutting-edge technologies in the field of computer science.
