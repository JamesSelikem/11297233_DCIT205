Insight Into MPhil and MSc Programmes

MPhil and Msc programmes

The Department of Computer Science – University of Ghana currently runs Masters of Science (MSc.) in Computer Science and Master of Philosophy (MPhil.) in Computer Science. The MSc. is a 1-year (weekend/evening) programme with a capstone project while the MPhil is a 2-year (full-time) programme. The first year of the MPhil is taught course and second year is research and dissertation. Both programmes are accelerated master’s programme specially designed to train current and future computer scientist who are available for full-time or part-time study.

MPhil. Computer Science: Level 600
First Semester

In the postgraduate program, our department focuses on advanced research and specialized knowledge. The first semester offers foundational courses like Research Methods and Advanced Data Structures and Algorithms. Principles of Wireless and Mobile Systems provide a deep dive into contemporary technologies. Elective choices include Advanced Computer Networks, Advanced Database Systems, Advanced Operating Systems, and Human-Computer Interaction, allowing students to tailor their studies to their research interests. This semester sets the stage for a rigorous exploration of cutting-edge concepts and methodologies, preparing students for leadership roles in academia, research, and industry.

Second semester

In the second semester of our postgraduate program, the focus shifts towards advanced applications and in-depth research. Advanced Software Engineering, Distributed Systems, and Seminar I provide a comprehensive understanding of cutting-edge technologies. Intelligent Systems further explores the realm of artificial intelligence. Electives such as Bioinformatics, Advanced Computer Vision, Wireless Systems Design, and Networks Security allow students to delve into specialized areas. The semester culminates with Computational Mathematics and the initiation of the MPhil Thesis, offering students an opportunity to contribute to the body of knowledge in computer science through original research. This semester prepares students for the culmination of their postgraduate journey.

MSc. Computer Science

The Department of Computer Science – University of Ghana currently runs Masters of Science (MSc.) in Computer Science and Master of Philosophy (MPhil.) in Computer Science. The MSc. is a 1-year (weekend/evening) programme with a capstone project while the MPhil is a 2-year (full-time) programme. The first year of the MPhil is taught course and second year is research and dissertation. Both programmes are accelerated master’s programme specially designed to train current and future computer scientist who are available for full-time or part-time study.

MSc. Computer Science
First Semester

In the initial semester of our postgraduate program, emphasis is placed on foundational research skills and advanced theoretical concepts. Courses like Research Methods and Advanced Data Structures and Algorithms equip students with the tools for scholarly inquiry. Principles of Wireless and Mobile Systems delve into modern communication technologies. Electives, including Advanced Computer Networks, Advanced Database Systems, Advanced Operating Systems, and Human-Computer Interaction, allow students to specialize based on their research interests. This semester sets the stage for rigorous academic exploration, providing a solid foundation for the postgraduate journey in computer science.

Second Semester

In the second semester of our postgraduate program, the focus shifts towards practical applications and advanced research. Advanced Software Engineering and Distributed Systems deepen students' understanding of software development and distributed computing. Seminar I offers a platform for scholarly discourse and presentation skills. Intelligent Systems explores the realms of artificial intelligence. Electives, including Bioinformatics, Advanced Computer Vision, Wireless Systems Design, and Networks Security, allow students to specialize. The semester concludes with a capstone MSc Dissertation, providing students with the opportunity to conduct original research and contribute to the academic discourse in their chosen area of computer science.
